October 7th 2018
  1. Idea. What is the Idea that you have. Has it been done? If so, can you do it better?
  2. What platform will you use?
    1. Do you want to target iOS and Android or just one of them
      1. If you want to target iOS only then you may want to consider Swift/Objective C in XCODE
        1. XCODE
      2. If you want to target Android only then you may want to consider Android Studio
        1. Android Studio
      3. Both
        1. CrossPlatform Tools
          1. Xamarin
          2. ReactNative
          3. Ionic
          4. PhoneGap
          5. Flutter
    2. Choosing a framework will come down to personal choice and research. For our purposes, we are using Xamarin as the tool. However, there are many options out there and you have to make the best desicision based on what your need is and what you need to accomplish. My reasoning for Xamarin, was due to my extensive .NET Framework Experience with C#. I can deliver applications for Mobile and Web using much of the same code base.
  3. Application Visuals
    1. How do you want to present your application?
    2. What do you want it to look like?
    3. What colors do you want?
    4.  What Icons do you want? .
    5. What images will you need?
  4. Let us take a look at several design sites, and preview current designs. Remember, that this is up to you an you must decide how you want to present your data in your application. Looking at designs and is one of the best ways to determine layout, images and colors.
    1. Inspired
    2. Mobile Patterns
    3. LovelyUI
    4. AndroidUX
  5. What tools to use in deciding and designing our Layout
    1. WireFrame
      1. This is the one we are using for our class. It is simple and easy.
    2. Examples of Others
      1. MavelApp

Starting a design can be very stressful. This is especially true when you know you want an app, and you have an idea but you don't know what to do to get there. So making a few decisions up front can help you start and to begin a plan of attack.
